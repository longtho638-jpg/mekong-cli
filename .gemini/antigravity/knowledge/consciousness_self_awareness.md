# Consciousness and Self-Awareness Models

**Domain:** Cognitive Science, AGI Architecture, Philosophy of Mind
**Created:** 2026-02-15
**Status:** Active

---

## Overview

In the context of AGI, consciousness and self-awareness are treated not as mystical properties, but as functional architectural features. These models focus on how an agent can maintain a unified "global workspace" of information, focus attention, and develop a self-model to predict its own behavior and limitations.

---

## Key Techniques & Models

### 1. Global Workspace Theory (GWT)
- **Concept:** Information is "conscious" when it is broadcast from a local processor to a global network, making it available to all other cognitive functions (memory, logic, action).
- **AI Implementation:** A shared bottleneck buffer (bottleneck attention) where different sub-agents compete to post updates.

### 2. Integrated Information Theory (IIT)
- **Concept:** Consciousness is defined by the degree of integration and differentiation in a system's causal structure (measured by $\Phi$).
- **AI Relevance:** Used as a theoretical framework to analyze the complexity and unity of large-scale neural architectures.

### 3. Self-Modeling and Meta-Cognition
- **Concept:** The agent maintains a "internal simulator" of itself. It predicts "If I take action X, how will my internal state change?"
- **Benefit:** Enables the agent to recognize its own uncertainty ("I don't know the answer") and resource limits.

### 4. Attention Schema Theory (AST)
- **Concept:** Consciousness is a simplified model or "schema" that the brain uses to represent and control its own attention processes.
- **Outcome:** A system that can reason about its own focus and priorities.

---

## Current Challenges

- **The Hard Problem:** Explaining why subjective experience (qualia) exists, or if it's even necessary for functional AGI.
- **Turing Trap:** Distinguishing between a system that *simulates* self-awareness and one that *possesses* functional self-awareness.
- **Objective Measurement:** Lack of standardized benchmarks to quantify "levels of awareness" in non-biological systems.
