# Interpretability and Explainable AI

**Domain:** XAI, Mechanistic Interpretability, Neural Analysis
**Created:** 2026-02-15
**Status:** Active

---

## Overview

Interpretability aims to open the "black box" of neural networks to understand the internal logic behind AI decisions. Modern research focuses on **Mechanistic Interpretability**, which seeks to map specific circuits and neurons to human-understandable concepts and features.

---

## Key Techniques & Models

### 1. Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs)
- **Concept:** A technique to decompose dense neural activations into a sparse set of high-level, interpretable "features" (e.g., a "Golden Gate Bridge" neuron or a "Code Debugging" circuit).
- **Application:** Used by Anthropic and OpenAI to map the internal mind of large models.

### 2. Activation Steering (Representation Engineering)
- **Concept:** Directly intervening in a model's latent space to push its behavior in a specific direction (e.g., increasing "honesty" or decreasing "sarcasm") during inference.
- **Benefit:** Provides control over model behavior without retraining.

### 3. Causal Tracing
- **Concept:** Identifying the exact locations (layers/neurons) where specific facts or reasoning patterns are stored by systematically corrupting and restoring activations.
- **Outcome:** A "map" of knowledge storage within the network.

### 4. Feature Visualization and Attribution
- **Concept:** Visualizing the patterns that cause specific neurons to fire.
- **Goal:** Understanding what a vision model "sees" or what a language model "associates" with specific tokens.

---

## Current Challenges

- **Superposition:** Individual neurons often represent multiple, unrelated concepts simultaneously, making them difficult to isolate.
- **Scale:** With billions of parameters, interpreting every circuit in a modern LLM is a massive computational and analytical undertaking.
- **Faithfulness:** Explanations generated by models (post-hoc rationalization) may not reflect the actual internal reasoning process.
